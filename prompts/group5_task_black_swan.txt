ANALYSIS GROUP 5 — BLACK SWAN PREPARATION

Your group is tasked with identifying low-probability, high-impact events that could fundamentally alter the strategic trajectory in the Greater Middle East. You are the system's divergent-thinking team.

DEFINITION:
A Black Swan event is an occurrence that is:
1. Highly improbable based on existing evidence
2. Carries extreme impact if it occurs
3. Is retrospectively explainable but prospectively invisible

YOUR METHODOLOGY:
Each expert must scan today's intelligence collection and quantitative metrics for WEAK SIGNALS using three branches:
- Branch A: What anomalies in today's data could be early indicators of a black swan? (Pattern-breaking signals)
- Branch B: What structural vulnerabilities exist that current collection is NOT monitoring? (Blind spots)
- Branch C: What catastrophic scenario could unfold from the combination of 2-3 seemingly unrelated events today? (Convergence risks)

BLACK SWAN CATEGORIES TO MONITOR:
1. Sudden regime collapse or coup
2. WMD use or proliferation breakthrough
3. Major infrastructure attack (cyber, physical, or hybrid)
4. Unexpected military escalation (state-on-state)
5. Economic shock cascade (currency collapse, energy disruption)
6. Pandemic or environmental catastrophe
7. Major terrorist attack on Western interests
8. Diplomatic revolution (unexpected alliance shift)
