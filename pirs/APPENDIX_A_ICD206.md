# Appendix A: ICD 206 Source Reliability and Information Credibility Reference

**Classification:** UNCLASSIFIED // OPEN SOURCE ONLY

Apply this framework when sourcing claims for senior military assessments. Require a minimum of two independent sources rated C or higher for any key judgment.

---

## Source Reliability Scale

| Rating | Criteria | Open-Source Examples |
|--------|----------|-------------------|
| A | Completely Reliable | CENTCOM press release, IAEA safeguards report, ACLED confirmed incident, DSCA arms sale notification |
| B | Usually Reliable | Reuters/AP wire, SOHR (with corroboration), INSS analysis, UKMTO incident report |
| C | Fairly Reliable | Al-Monitor, ICG Crisis Watch, Middle East Eye, OPSGROUP aviation reports |
| D | Not Usually Reliable | State media (IRNA, SANA, Saba News), unverified social media, IAMG official statements |
| E | Unreliable | Unverified Telegram channels, anonymous accounts without geolocation corroboration |
| F | Reliability Unknown | New or untested sources with no track record |

---

## Information Credibility Scale

| Rating | Criteria | Analytic Application |
|--------|----------|---------------------|
| 1 | Confirmed | Confirmed by independent sources or directly observed; physically verified by satellite or on-ground reporting |
| 2 | Probably True | Consistent with established patterns; largely corroborated by at least two C-rated or higher sources |
| 3 | Possibly True | Plausible; corroborated by other uncorroborated sources; consistent with actor historical behavior |
| 4 | Doubtful | Implausible; inconsistent with known activity patterns; sourced from single D or E-rated origin |
| 5 | Improbable | Contradicted by reliable sources; imagery or other physical evidence does not support claim |
| 6 | Cannot Be Judged | Insufficient data to evaluate; single source with no corroboration path available |

---

## Key Rules for This Collection Framework

1. Never cite a single source for a key judgment. Require minimum two independent sources at C or higher.

2. State media (D-rating) is useful for identifying official positions but requires independent corroboration for any factual claim.

3. Social media is always D or E unless geolocated and cross-referenced with imagery or wire reporting.

4. GPS jamming (GPSJam.org) is an indicator, not proof of kinetic activity; correlate with NASA FIRMS, wire reports, and official press.

5. ACLED incidents are coded by trained analysts; treat as B for geolocation and timing, C for casualty figures.

6. IAEA reports on Iran enrichment are A for declared facility status; undeclared activities require separate corroboration from CNS or ISIS satellite analysis.
