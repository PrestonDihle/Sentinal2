# PIR 3: Stabilizing Forces and De-escalation Indicators

**Classification:** UNCLASSIFIED // OPEN SOURCE ONLY

**PIR Question:** What factors are currently operating to restrain escalation or restore stability, and do these factors indicate a durable reduction in conflict risk? This PIR is required to answer the Big Question's bidirectional framing -- the framework must be able to detect both deterioration and improvement. Without this PIR, the collection architecture produces consistently one-sided analytical judgments regardless of ground truth.

---

## Indicator 3.1: Active Diplomatic Mediation and Back-Channel Resumption

**Purpose:** Functioning back-channel diplomacy is the primary restraint mechanism in US-Iran crisis management; observable indicators of mediation health directly answer the stabilization question.

### SIR 3.1.1
**Requirement:** Monitor Omani and Qatari foreign ministry public statements, US State Department readout releases, and wire service reporting from regional diplomatic hubs for language indicating active mediation of US-Iran or Iran-Israel indirect communications, humanitarian pause negotiations, or prisoner exchange frameworks.

**Collection Method:** Monitor official diplomatic sources and Gulf-focused regional press for mediation language and back-channel signaling; cross-reference US and partner government readout content.

**Primary Sources:**
- Omani Ministry of Foreign Affairs (mofa.gov.om) -- Oman hosts the primary US-Iran back-channel; watch for senior visitor announcements, joint communiques, and diplomatic travel patterns
- Qatar News Agency (qna.org.qa/en) -- Qatar hosts Hamas political bureau and serves as US-facilitated MENA diplomatic node; official wire for mediation milestone reporting
- US State Department Press Briefings (state.gov/press-briefings) -- official US acknowledgment of mediation progress; readout language changes are analytically significant
- Reuters Gulf Desk and AP Middle East (reuters.com/world/middle-east) -- most reliable wire for breaking diplomatic developments; cross-reference for corroboration
- Al-Monitor Diplomacy Coverage (al-monitor.com) -- policy-focused; Iran and Gulf diplomacy tracking; covers back-channel developments with sourced analytical context

### SIR 3.1.2
**Requirement:** Report any resumption of IAEA inspector access to Iranian declared or previously restricted nuclear facilities following a period of restricted access, or any Iranian notification of reduced enrichment levels at Fordow or Natanz, as an indicator of diplomatic stabilization on the nuclear track that materially reduces near-term conflict risk.

**Collection Method:** Monitor IAEA Director General quarterly reports and Board of Governors statements; cross-reference with arms control analysis community for interpretation.

**Primary Sources:**
- IAEA Iran Safeguards (iaea.org/topics/iran) -- primary authoritative source; quarterly reports on inspector access status and enrichment levels; A-rating for declared facility status
- Arms Control Association (armscontrol.org) -- tracks JCPOA compliance and diplomatic milestones; publishes rapid analysis of IAEA developments with policy context
- ISIS (isis-online.org) -- provides open-source interpretation of IAEA findings with facility-specific imagery and technical context
- Arms Control Wonk (armscontrolwonk.com) -- technical analysis; often first to publicly interpret IAEA report language in analytically accessible terms
- Reuters Vienna Desk (reuters.com) -- covers IAEA Board of Governors meetings with rapid reporting; cross-reference official IAEA text

## Indicator 3.2: Reduction in IAMG Operational Tempo

**Purpose:** A sustained reduction in IAMG attack frequency -- as opposed to a temporary tactical pause -- is the primary military indicator of de-escalation below the kinetic threshold.

### SIR 3.2.1
**Requirement:** Monitor ACLED Iraq and ACLED Syria for a sustained 30-day reduction in IAMG-attributed rocket, mortar, and drone SIGACT frequency compared to the prior 90-day baseline, corroborated by CENTCOM press releases and regional wire reporting. Distinguish between a deliberate operational stand-down and a temporary tactical pause.

**Collection Method:** Weekly ACLED data download and trend analysis; cross-reference with US military reporting and regional press for corroboration and intent assessment.

**Primary Sources:**
- ACLED Iraq and Syria (acleddata.com) -- geolocated SIGACT database; filter by IAMG-affiliated actor groups; exportable CSV and GeoJSON; B-rating for geolocation and timing; update weekly
- CENTCOM Press Releases (centcom.mil/MEDIA/PRESS-RELEASES) -- official record of force protection incidents and response operations; cross-reference against ACLED for frequency trends
- OIR Operation Inherent Resolve (inherentresolve.mil) -- Iraq and Syria coalition press; airstrike and SIGACT updates with actor attribution
- Shafaq News (shafaq.com/en) -- covers PMF and IAMG activity in Iraq with good sub-provincial granularity; useful for local trend detection
- SOHR (syriahr.com/en) -- raw incident data for Syrian-side IAMG activity; cross-reference for completeness

### SIR 3.2.2
**Requirement:** Observe any public statements by prominent IAMG leadership from Kata'ib Hezbollah, Harakat al-Nujaba, or the Iraqi Islamic Resistance via their official Telegram channels and affiliated media indicating operational pause, strategic patience framing, or acknowledgment of ceasefire compliance. Such framing historically precedes negotiated stand-down periods.

**Collection Method:** Monitor IAMG official Telegram channels and affiliated Arabic-language media for leadership framing language; cross-reference with MEMRI translations for non-Arabic readers.

**Primary Sources:**
- tgstat.com -- search for Kata'ib Hezbollah, Harakat al-Nujaba, and Islamic Resistance in Iraq affiliated channels; monitor leadership statement frequency and framing tone shifts
- Iraqi PMF Official Media (pmu.gov.iq) -- official Popular Mobilization Forces communications; political and operational framing at the coalition level
- Al Furat News (alfuratnews.com) -- Shia-affiliated; PMF-friendly; often carries IAMG leadership statements ahead of other platforms
- MEMRI (memri.org) -- English translations of IAMG leadership statements; essential for non-Arabic readers monitoring framing shifts from operational to political language
- Al Mayadeen (english.almayadeen.net) -- Resistance Axis platform; publishes IAMG leadership interviews and communiques with regional framing context

## Indicator 3.3: Economic Normalization Signals

**Purpose:** Iranian economic stabilization reduces the domestic pressure driving externally-directed aggression; Saudi-Iran normalization reduces the permissive environment for IAMG operations.

### SIR 3.3.1
**Requirement:** Monitor Iran's unofficial US dollar parallel exchange rate for sustained rial appreciation and the Tehran Stock Exchange (TEDPIX index) for equity market recovery trends, which indicate reduced domestic economic pressure on the Iranian regime and potentially reduced incentive for externally-directed escalation as a distraction strategy.

**Collection Method:** Daily tracking of Iran parallel FX rate; weekly TEDPIX trend analysis; cross-reference with domestic Iranian media for regime response framing.

**Primary Sources:**
- BonBast.com (bonbast.com) -- Iran's unofficial USD/IRR exchange rate; updated multiple times daily; rial appreciation sustained over 2-4 weeks is a leading stabilization indicator
- Tehran Stock Exchange TEDPIX (tse.ir/en) -- equity market recovery reflects domestic economic confidence and expectations of sanctions relief or conflict termination
- Iran International (iranintl.com/en) -- covers Iranian economic developments including FX rate trends and their political implications; fastest English-language source
- Trading Economics Iran (tradingeconomics.com) -- aggregated macroeconomic data; inflation and currency trend contextualization and historical comparison
- World Bank Iran Data (data.worldbank.org) -- quarterly economic updates; lagged but authoritative for structural economic trend assessment

### SIR 3.3.2
**Requirement:** Report observable progress in Saudi-Iran normalization implementation -- specifically joint economic commission meetings, ambassador-level exchanges, and trade delegation activity -- via Saudi SPA wire, IRNA, and Gulf affairs press, as indicators of strategic competition management between the two primary regional powers that reduces the operational permissive environment for IAMG escalation.

**Collection Method:** Monitor Saudi and Iranian official media and Gulf affairs press for normalization milestone reporting; cross-reference both sides for consistent acknowledgment.

**Primary Sources:**
- Saudi Press Agency (spa.gov.sa/en) -- official KSA state wire; normalization announcements and bilateral commission meeting outcomes; A-rating for Saudi-side official statements
- IRNA (en.irna.ir) -- official Iranian state wire; Saudi-Iran normalization coverage from Tehran's perspective; cross-reference with SPA for mutual acknowledgment
- Al-Monitor Gulf Pulse (al-monitor.com) -- policy-focused; Iran-Saudi normalization tracking with analytical depth and sourcing from both sides
- Asharq Al-Awsat (english.aawsat.com) -- Saudi-owned pan-Arab paper; strong on Gulf diplomatic affairs; covers normalization milestones with regional context
- Carnegie Middle East Center (carnegie-mec.org) -- Beirut-based; publishes analysis on Saudi-Iran normalization implications for Lebanon and Syria IAMG dynamics

## Indicator 3.4: International Community Stabilization Measures

**Purpose:** UN Security Council action and international financial institution engagement are lagging but authoritative indicators of international consensus on conflict limitation, and signal the availability of stabilization mechanisms.

### SIR 3.4.1
**Requirement:** Monitor UN Security Council meeting schedules, outcome documents, and UN Secretary General statements for language establishing ceasefire monitoring frameworks, humanitarian corridor agreements, or maritime security arrangements for the Red Sea or Persian Gulf that would indicate international community convergence on conflict limitation mechanisms.

**Collection Method:** Monitor UN official channels for Security Council resolutions and presidential statements; cross-reference with Reuters UN desk for rapid reporting.

**Primary Sources:**
- UN Security Council (un.org/securitycouncil/content/resolutions) -- all UNSC resolutions and presidential statements; primary authoritative source for international community formal positions
- UN Secretary General Press Statements (un.org/sg/en/content/sg/press-release) -- real-time SG positioning on conflict escalation and ceasefire proposals
- UNIFIL Lebanon (unifil.unmissions.org) -- UN Lebanon Force reports; ceasefire violation documentation under UNSC Resolution 1701; status of Blue Line monitoring
- UNAMI Iraq Mission (unami.unmissions.org) -- UN Iraq political and security reporting; UNSC mandates for the Iraq theater
- Reuters UN Desk (reuters.com) -- covers Security Council sessions with rapid and sourced reporting; cross-reference for official document context

### SIR 3.4.2
**Requirement:** Monitor IMF and World Bank emergency lending activations, standby arrangements, or rapid financing instruments deployed for conflict-affected regional states (Lebanon, Jordan, Yemen, or Gaza) as indicators of international community commitment to preventing economic collapse cascades that would deepen and prolong regional instability.

**Collection Method:** Monitor IMF and World Bank press release databases for MENA country emergency financial measures; cross-reference with ACAPS crisis severity scoring.

**Primary Sources:**
- IMF Press Releases (imf.org/en/News/Articles) -- country-specific lending arrangements and emergency finance activations; formal indicator of international commitment to economic stabilization
- World Bank MENA Operations (worldbank.org/en/region/mena) -- emergency lending, grants, and development finance for conflict-affected states; program approval announcements
- USAID Economic Reports (usaid.gov/middle-east) -- US bilateral economic stabilization funding; advance indicator of US commitment to preventing regional economic collapse
- ACAPS (acaps.org) -- tracks humanitarian funding gaps and economic shock severity across MENA crisis states; cross-reference for context on whether IFI funding is sufficient
- Fitch Ratings free summaries (fitchratings.com/research/sovereigns) -- sovereign credit downgrades in MENA states serve as stabilization failure indicators
